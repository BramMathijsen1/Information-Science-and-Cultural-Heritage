Hattori Hanzō

Hattori Hanzō (c. 1542 – 1597) was a samurai (侍) and tactical expert who served the Tokugawa clan, earning the nickname Oni no Hanzō (鬼の半蔵, Demon Hanzō) for saving the life of Tokugawa Ieyasu and helping him unify Japan. As a commander of ninja forces trained in Iga Province, he became one of the most famous men ever called Iga no mono (伊賀の者) — "a person from Iga".

Siege of Kaminogō Castle

In 1562, early in Matsudaira Motoyasu's (the future Tokugawa Ieyasu's) revolt against Imagawa dominance, Hattori Hanzō led a small band of ninja against Kaminogō Castle. Hanzō is credited with rescuing Ieyasu's hostage wife and children from the Imagawa garrison there, in one of the few operations attributed to him that historical sources, rather than legend alone, actually record.

Ninja

A ninja was a spy and infiltrator of pre-modern Japan, a mercenary who gathered intelligence and carried out sabotage. Regional terms such as Iga-mono (伊賀者) identified these operatives by their origin, and the Iga and Kōga (甲賀) regions became especially renowned for training them during the Sengoku period (戦国時代).

Ninjutsu

Ninjutsu (忍術) encompasses the techniques and skills used by the ninja — irregular warfare, intelligence gathering, and above all methods to avoid detection and to escape, taught and preserved within the Iga-ryū tradition.

Iga Province

Iga Province, in what is now western Mie Prefecture, was rather inaccessible because of its mountainous terrain and poor roads. This isolation let the region become a centre of ninjutsu activity, one of the birthplaces of the ninja clans alongside Kōga.

Iga-ryū

Iga-ryū (伊賀流) is an umbrella term, according to Japanese legend, for the ninjutsu traditions that came from the Iga region, in what is now Iga City and Nabari City in Mie Prefecture.

Bansenshūkai

The Bansenshūkai (万川集海), compiled in 1676 by Fujibayashi Yasutake, gathered the ninjutsu knowledge of the Iga and Kōga clans into a single text, preserving tactical expertise developed over Japan's long period of conflict. The text was made to preserve the art of Ninjutsu in a period of peace where it was no longer needed.

Mizugumo

The mizugumo (水蜘蛛), or "water spider", was a water-crossing device said to have been used by the ninja to cross moats, rivers, and other bodies of water undetected.

Kunai

The kunai was originally a farming and masonry tool. Its low cost and wide availability made it easy for the ninja to adapt into a concealable weapon, giving it its iconic place in ninja equipment.

Ninja Museum of Igaryu

The Ninja Museum of Igaryu (伊賀流忍者博物館), in Iga, Mie Prefecture, is dedicated to the history of the ninja and displays over four hundred ninja tools and weapons, including kunai and shuriken (手裏剣).

Ghost of Tsushima

Ghost of Tsushima, released in 2020 by Sucker Punch Productions, lets its samurai protagonist choose between honourable combat and the dishonourable "Ghost" stealth tactics long associated with the ninja, keeping the legend alive in contemporary popular culture.
